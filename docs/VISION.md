# Vision — Socratic Writer

## Core Intent

Create the purest possible digital writing instrument for serious creative and reflective work.

The tool must feel less like "software" and more like a high-quality blank page that happens to have an invisible, wise companion who only speaks when invited and only in the form of genuine questions.

## What "Ultra Minimalist" Really Means

- **Zero visual clutter** at all times. No toolbar, no status bar (unless optionally summoned), no word count, no formatting controls, no theme switcher in the moment of writing.
- **Zero cognitive load** for the act of writing itself. The interface must disappear.
- **Zero decision fatigue**. You never ask "what button do I press now?" You only ever write or ask for reflection.
- **Zero features that serve the tool instead of the writer**. No export anxiety during creation. No "save" concept that interrupts.
- **Radical simplicity of input**: Only two modes of interaction — typing to write, and the space bar for the single essential meta-action.

## The Socratic Component

The LLM does **not**:
- Complete your sentences
- Suggest "better" words
- Generate plot ideas or content for you
- Act as a co-author

The LLM **does**:
- Surface the implicit structure in what you have already written
- Ask precise, Socratic questions that help you discover what you actually think
- Point out tensions, assumptions, gaps, or underdeveloped threads
- Help you see the architecture of your own ideas so you can restructure them yourself

This is "maieutic" (midwifery) rather than generative AI. The goal is deeper thought, not faster output.

## The Writing Experience We Are Designing For

Imagine sitting down with a truly excellent notebook and a pen that never runs out. The page is infinite. There is no back button. There is no way to "fix" yesterday's sentence except by writing a better one today.

Then imagine that every time you press the space bar (perhaps after finishing a paragraph or when you feel stuck), a small, elegant margin note or quiet overlay appears containing 1–3 penetrating questions that make you pause and think more clearly — without ever breaking your ability to keep typing forward.

You can ignore the questions completely and continue writing. Or you can let one of them reshape the next ten minutes of thinking.

## Non-Goals (Important)

- This is **not** a general-purpose text editor.
- This is **not** a note-taking app.
- This is **not** an AI writing assistant in the current market sense (no "make it longer", "make it funnier", "continue in the style of...").
- This is **not** a tool for producing finished, publishable documents in one session.
- This is **not** collaborative.

## Success Criteria (How We Will Know We Got It Right)

1. A writer can open the tool and be typing meaningful prose within 3 seconds of launch, with zero configuration.
2. After 45 minutes of use, the writer reports they "forgot the software existed" and were only aware of their thoughts.
3. The Socratic prompts, when used, produce a noticeable "oh, I didn't realize I was thinking that" moment.
4. The writer never feels the urge to reach for the mouse or function keys while in flow.
5. Re-reading sessions feel like re-reading a high-quality physical journal, not "using an app."

## Long-Term Dream

A small number of people who do deep intellectual or creative work consider this their primary writing environment — the place they go when the thinking is hard and they want to do it well.

It becomes culturally known as "the serious writer's thinking tool" rather than "yet another writing app."
